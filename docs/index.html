**The English Poem**

O, fair moors of Aalborg, how thy chill grasp doth cling,
A biting frost that doth my very soul sting.
The winds, they howl like restless spirits, wild and free,
A gale that doth my cloak and hat from my shoulders rip.

Yet, in Copenhagen's streets, a milder climate doth prevail,
A gentle warmth that doth the heart and senses regale.
But soft, what whispers of rain I hear, a quiet patter,
Faint as a lover's sigh, in the darkness of the evening's shadowy weather.

Kathmandu's sun-kissed heights, a radiant warmth that doth ascend,
A blazing heat that doth the earth and all its creatures amend.
A languid breeze, like a contented lover's gentle caress,
Stirs not a blade of grass, in the sun-baked plains' torrid cauldron's stress.

O, which place to call home for a night, for a fleeting stay?
Aalborg's biting frost, though piercing, holds a certain grim allure.
Copenhagen's gentle warmth may soothe the soul, but it holds naught of might.
Kathmandu's fiery heat, ah, that doth stir within me a longing, a deep desire, to bask in its golden light.

Tomorrow, I would most like to roam the Kathmandu plains, to lose myself in its radiant splendor, to feel the warmth on my face, to dance under its golden sun.
