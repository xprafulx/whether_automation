**THE Nepali POEM (म ताप्ति नागली ना) 

म ताप्ति नागली ना,
धिक्कारिने स्काइ माझो
कालो सायं धिक्कारिने,
चौमासिने स्काइ माझो.

वातावरणमा थपकाय,
म तपकाय तपली माझो
जलधि बारिश पिउन,
म तपला जितलो माझो.

माझो ताप्ति वासदार,
काठमाडोका माझो ताप्ति
वातावरणमा खेलै,
जलधि र सूर्य समातै.

म हाम्रो मित्रले कहाँ,
कित्ति जागले पाछे माझो?
अहिलो माझो ताप्ति, र,
म नागले काढे खेलै.

Translation (Literal):

I am an outdoor enthusiast,
My heart is entwined with this sky, 
At night the sky is gloomy, 
But in summer, the sky is cheerful.

There is a gentle whisper in the atmosphere,
I am enchanted by this gentle breeze,
I drink in the sound of raindrops,
And I am at peace in this place.

My friend's city of Aalborg,
I don't like his city's chill,
He tells me about the wind and the sky,
And the city of summer is cheerful.

He asks me, "My friend, where will I go?"
I tell him, "Go to the warm land of Kathmandu",
Now my friend's heart is excited, and he says,
"Tomorrow I will go and play with the sun".

The use of "काठमाडोका माझो ताप्ति" (the warmth of Kathmandu) highlights the poet's preference for warm and sunny weather. The final lines of the poem, "अहिलो माझो ताप्ति, र, म नागले काढे खेलै" (Now my friend's heart is excited, and he says, "Tomorrow I will go and play with the sun"), emphasizes the poet's enthusiasm for outdoor activities and their love for nature.
